The Problem with Platform-Native Reporting

Every morning starts the same way: log into Google Ads, check performance. Switch to Meta Ads, check performance. Open LinkedIn, check performance. Maybe pull some data into a spreadsheet. Compare numbers manually. Try to get a sense of what's actually working.

Each advertising platform has its own marketing analytics dashboard, its own metrics definitions, its own date formats. Google Ads shows you one story, Meta tells another, and neither gives you the complete picture. You're left piecing together fragmented data from multiple sources just to answer simple questions.

"How did we perform this week?" shouldn't require opening five browser tabs and a spreadsheet. "Which channel is driving the best results?" shouldn't take 30 minutes to figure out. Yet that's the reality for most performance marketers managing campaigns across platforms.

The data exists. It's just scattered across systems that were never designed to work together. What you need is a unified marketing dashboard software that brings it all together—without the complexity of enterprise BI tools or the limitations of basic reporting.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a marketing dashboard?

A marketing dashboard is software that brings together data from multiple advertising and analytics platforms into one unified view. Instead of logging into Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads, and Google Analytics separately, a marketing dashboard shows your key metrics in a single place, making it easier to understand overall performance and spot trends.

How is Analytics Reporter different from Google Analytics?

Google Analytics focuses on website behavior—page views, sessions, user paths. Analytics Reporter focuses on campaign performance across ad platforms. It answers "how are my campaigns performing?" rather than "what are users doing on my site?" Both are valuable, but serve different purposes in your marketing stack.

Which platforms does the marketing dashboard connect to?

Analytics Reporter connects to Google Ads, Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram), and LinkedIn Ads. This covers the most common paid advertising channels for performance marketers. Additional platform integrations are planned based on user feedback.

Can I customize which metrics appear on my dashboard?

Yes. While Analytics Reporter comes with sensible defaults (spend, conversions, cost per conversion, ROAS), you can customize which metrics appear and how they're prioritized. The goal is showing you what matters for your specific campaigns, not overwhelming you with every possible data point.

How often does the marketing dashboard update?

Analytics Reporter syncs with your ad platforms daily, pulling the latest performance data each morning. This gives you fresh data for your daily performance review without the delays of manual data exports or the complexity of real-time streaming.

The hidden cost of manual ad spend tracking

Every month, performance marketers face the same challenge: keeping track of ad spend across multiple platforms. Google Ads, Meta Ads, LinkedIn Ads. Each with its own dashboard, its own reporting lag, its own way of calculating budgets.

The result? You either spend hours each week updating spreadsheets, or you get surprised at month-end when a campaign has overspent by 40%. Neither option is good for your results or your sanity.

Platform-native budget tools don't solve this. They only show you one channel at a time. They don't account for your total marketing budget or how spend in one platform affects your ability to spend in another.

One view of all your ad spend

Budget Checker gives you a single view of your ad spend across all platforms. Set your monthly budgets once, connect your ad accounts, and get a clear picture of where you stand every day.

No more logging into three platforms to piece together your spend. No more spreadsheet formulas that break when someone adds a new campaign. Just open Budget Checker, glance at your pacing, and know exactly where you stand.

How the ad spend tracker works

Budget Checker is designed for daily operational checks, not endless data exploration.

1

Connect your ad platforms

Link your Google Ads, Meta Ads, and LinkedIn Ads accounts in minutes. Budget Checker pulls spend data automatically, so you always have the latest numbers.

2

Set your monthly budgets

Define your budget for each platform. Budget Checker calculates where you should be based on the day of the month and compares it to actual spend.

3

Check daily, act early

Open Budget Checker each morning for a quick budget check. Green means on track. Yellow means pacing high. Red means action needed. Simple.

Frequently asked questions

What is an ad spend tracker?

An ad spend tracker is a tool that monitors your advertising budget across multiple platforms like Google Ads, Meta Ads, and LinkedIn Ads. It shows you how much you've spent versus your budget, calculates pacing, and alerts you when spending patterns indicate potential overspend or underspend.

Why do I need an ad spend tracker instead of checking each platform?

Checking each ad platform individually is time-consuming and error-prone. Platforms don't show your overall budget picture or how spend across channels affects your total marketing budget. An ad spend tracker like Budget Checker gives you a unified view, saves time on daily checks, and catches budget issues before they become problems.

How does Budget Checker calculate budget pacing?

Budget Checker calculates pacing by comparing your actual spend to where you should be based on the day of the month. For example, if you're 15 days into a 30-day month, you should have spent roughly 50% of your budget. If you've spent 70%, Budget Checker flags this as "pacing high" so you can adjust before overspending.

Which ad platforms does Budget Checker support?

Budget Checker currently supports Google Ads, Meta Ads (Facebook and Instagram), and LinkedIn Ads. These are the most commonly used platforms for performance marketing. Additional platform support is planned based on user feedback.

How often does Budget Checker update spend data?

Budget Checker syncs with your ad platforms daily, pulling the latest spend data each morning. This ensures you have accurate, up-to-date information for your daily budget check without the lag of manual reporting.
